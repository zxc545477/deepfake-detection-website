How our neural forensics work

An end‑to‑end pipeline that combines face‑aware preprocessing, dual‑stream CNNs, frequency analysis, and temporal modeling to distinguish real from manipulated media with high accuracy.

error Problem Statement

With the rapid advancement of AI and deep learning, deepfake images and videos have become increasingly realistic, posing serious threats such as misinformation, identity misuse, and digital fraud. Traditional detection methods are insufficient to identify sophisticated deepfakes generated using modern neural networks. Hence, there is a strong need for an automated, reliable, and efficient deepfake detection system that can accurately distinguish between real and manipulated facial media.

⚡ high‑impact features

stream

Dual‑stream detection

RGB spatial CNN (Xception / ResNet) + frequency‑domain transformer to capture GAN / upsampling artifacts.

timeline

Temporal consistency module

For videos: short‑term frame differencing + temporal CNN / LSTM to detect frame‑level anomalies.

face

Face alignment + multi‑crop

Detect & normalize faces, examine full face, eyes, mouth crops to expose localised forgeries.

database

Cross‑dataset training

Trained on FaceForensics++, Celeb‑DF, DFDC for strong generalization.

Grad‑CAM/attention visualizations and per‑frame confidence timeline for forensic review.

📊 training & evaluation strategy

Datasets

  • FaceForensics++ (Rössler et al., 2019)
  • Celeb-DF (Li et al., 2020)
  • DeepFake Detection Challenge (DFDC)

Cross-dataset holdout to measure generalization.

Metrics

  • AUC-ROC, Accuracy, F1, Equal Error Rate (EER)
  • Per-clip and per-frame scores
  • Leave-one-dataset-out validation

Losses & robustness

Binary cross‑entropy + focal loss for class imbalance; adversarial training; multiple compression levels; blur/noise augmentation.
